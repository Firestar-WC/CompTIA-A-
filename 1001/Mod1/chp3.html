Special Function Keys

  • Tablets often have special keys used for special features; however they vary from maker to model
  • See chart below:
    Key Function
    Wireless Capability Turns wireless connection off and on
    Cellular Turns cellular network off and on (most likely on a tablet)
    Mute Mutes or unmutes audio
    Volume (increase or decrease) Increases and decreases audio volume respectively
    Brightness (increase or decrease) Increases and decreases screen brightness respectively
    Dual Display Controls multiple display outputs (for mobile devices connected to a laptop or desktop externally via cable)

More Special Function Keys

  • For laptops with Bluetooth, there might be a corresponding key (or keys) that pair and unpair.
  • There also might be a certain key combination to turn on the backlight (the light that shines on a keyboard when its dark, allowing key visibility).
  • There can also be a key for disabling touchpad or changing orientation (check F keys).
  • Depending on brand, there might even be a fast forward, rewind, pause, GPS, and even an airplane mode key.
  • When in doubt, check the keyboard manual.

Expanders and Locks

  • There are many external things to add to a laptop.
    • Docking stations - makes space to give laptop a feel of a desktop; can hold monitor, keyboard, mouse, network card, and other expansion slots. NOT A PORT REPLICATOR
    • Port replicators - only add ports, not explansion slots; includes USP ports, sound ports, and network.
    • Cable locks - similar to bike locks in function, a cable with one end as a lock and the other as a key. Set the combination to both lock and unlock it.
